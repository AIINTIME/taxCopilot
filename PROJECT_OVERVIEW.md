# TaxAI Platform Overview

## The Short Pitch

TaxAI is an AI-powered tax workbench for individuals, firms, finance teams, and administrators who need faster, more organized, and more secure tax analysis. It brings together focused tax workflows, document-aware chat, multi-model research, project workspaces, access control, audit visibility, and an admin console into one modern platform.

The product is designed around a simple idea: tax work should not live across disconnected files, emails, spreadsheets, and generic chat windows. TaxAI gives each tax task a dedicated workspace, lets users upload the right evidence, ask natural-language questions, and move from analysis to action with more confidence.

This document reflects the product capabilities surfaced in the current UI. Some areas are presented as demo or preview experiences, but they represent the intended platform surface and customer value.

## What TaxAI Is

TaxAI is a secure AI tax copilot and operations platform. It helps teams:

- Analyse personal income tax situations.
- Review corporate tax positions.
- Calculate and reason through capital gains.
- Understand government notices and prepare structured replies.
- Compare tax-law provisions and product impact.
- Run deeper multi-model research.
- Organize reusable project knowledge spaces.
- Manage users, roles, permissions, documents, audit logs, token usage, and system settings from an admin console.

At the user level, TaxAI feels like a focused tax assistant. At the organization level, it becomes a governed tax operations platform.

## Who It Is For

TaxAI is built for:

- Chartered accountants and tax consultants handling multiple client matters.
- Corporate finance and tax teams reviewing filings, notices, and compliance risks.
- Individuals who need guidance around salary, investments, deductions, regimes, and gains.
- Legal, compliance, and audit teams that need visibility into tax research and AI-assisted decisions.
- Administrators who need controlled access, role-based permissions, document oversight, and usage monitoring.

## Core User Experience

### AI Tax Copilot Dashboard

The main dashboard introduces TaxAI as an AI tax copilot and lets users choose the right workflow before they begin. Instead of starting with a blank generic assistant, users begin with a tax-specific path.

The dashboard currently highlights four AI workflows:

- Personal Income Tax
- Corporate Tax
- Capital Gains
- Government Notice Assistant

It also includes tax highlight cards, giving the product a news-and-insights layer that can later connect to a dynamic tax content feed.

### Focused Workflow-Based Chat

Each workflow opens a dedicated chat workspace. The interface is tuned to the type of tax task being handled, including the prompt guidance, upload hints, accepted documents, and expected outputs.

Users can:

- Start new chats.
- Continue recent chats.
- Rename chats.
- Delete chats.
- Search chat history.
- Upload supporting files into the workspace.
- Ask natural-language tax questions.
- Receive structured AI output such as summaries, warnings, drafts, tables, suggestions, metrics, and comparison cards.

This creates a tax-first experience rather than a generic chat experience.

## Tax Workflows

### Personal Income Tax

The Personal Income Tax workflow is designed for salary, investment, deduction, and regime analysis.

It supports use cases such as:

- Reviewing Form 16, AIS, investment proofs, rent receipts, and broker statements.
- Understanding HRA, deductions, and capital gains interactions.
- Comparing old and new regime fit.
- Identifying missing documents before filing.
- Summarizing a taxpayer's position in plain language.

### Corporate Tax

The Corporate Tax workflow is built for company-level review and optimization.

It supports use cases such as:

- Reviewing balance sheets and profit-and-loss statements.
- Checking GST data, audit notes, and schedules.
- Identifying compliance issues.
- Exploring optimization paths.
- Supporting finance teams with document-backed tax analysis.

### Capital Gains

The Capital Gains workflow focuses on asset sales, exemptions, and calculation support.

It supports use cases such as:

- Reviewing broker statements.
- Analysing property sale documents.
- Evaluating exemption eligibility.
- Checking reinvestment scenarios.
- Explaining tax impact around capital gains events.

### Government Notice Assistant

The Notice Assistant helps users understand official communications and draft structured responses.

It supports two clear evidence lanes:

- Government notice: notice, demand letter, order, or official communication.
- Supporting documents: ITRs, bank records, proofs, ledgers, replies, and other evidence.

This workflow is designed to help users:

- Understand what a notice is asking for.
- Identify evidence needed for response.
- Draft structured replies.
- Separate government claims from taxpayer support.
- Reduce deadline and documentation risk.

## Deep Research

TaxAI includes a Deep Research experience for comparing multiple AI model answers side by side.

Users can:

- Select one or more models.
- Submit a single research prompt.
- Compare summaries, key points, and caveats across model responses.
- Use the results to form a more balanced tax view.

This is especially useful for complex issues where a team wants to compare reasoning paths, identify uncertainty, and avoid relying on a single answer.

## IT Act Comparison

TaxAI includes an IT Act Comparison module that presents provision-by-provision comparisons and explains product impact.

The UI is designed to show:

- Earlier legal position.

- Automation fit.

This positions TaxAI as more than a Q&A tool. It becomes a platform for understanding how tax-law changes affect workflows, filing choices, notices, and internal decision logic.

## Example Customer Story

A finance team receives a government notice. They open the Notice Assistant, upload the notice in one lane and supporting documents in another. TaxAI helps explain what the department is asking for, identifies missing evidence, and drafts a structured reply.

At the same time, the team creates a project knowledge space for the matter. They upload prior filings, bank records, and correspondence. Persistent instructions tell the assistant to use uploaded files as the source of truth and call out assumptions.

An administrator reviews access, confirms the right people have the right roles, and later checks audit activity and AI usage. Management can see notice deadline risks and workflow volume in analytics.

The result is a more organized, traceable, and collaborative tax response process.
